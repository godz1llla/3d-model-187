Task. The three brothers were going to install a wireless router that occupies an area in a forest park. The plots have the same size. Each section has a length of 100 meters and is located in a triangle (Figure 15):

 
Figure 15. The shape of the forest park

The brothers decided to save the Internet and install one router for three area. To find a point that is equally close to all areas, they tightly twisted three squares in a circle and placed the router directly in the center of the resulting circle (Figure 16). But the brothers did not take into account that the router's signal reaches only 130 meters, and the Internet is lost beyond it. Will this be enough for router brothers or do you need to get a stronger one?

 
Figure 16. Configuring the router  

Solution: To understand if this router is enough for the brothers, you need to know what the radius of their circle is. If the radius is less than the coverage radius of the router, then everything is fine, but if not, then you will have to change the router.
To do this, first draw three diameters through the circle, each of which passes through the vertex of the triangle. Each side of the triangle is also 100 meters long, because it consists of the sides of a square:

 
Figure 17. Circumference diameters

Since all three straight lines of an equilateral triangle intersect at one point, each straight line and bisector (bisects the angle) and median (bisects the opposite wall) also run at right angles to the opposite wall (Figure 17). Since the plots are square, this line also crosses the opposite side at right angles and divides the left side in half.
 
Figure 18. Division of the central triangle

Now let's look at the central triangle, or rather, closer to one of the formed small triangles (Figure 18). Since each blue line is a bisector, and in an equilateral triangle all angles are 60°, the left angle is 30°. We know that the sum of all the angles of a triangle is 180°, but we already have two angles - 30° and 90°. This is the upper corner 180° − 90° − 30° = 60° means that it will be equal (Figure 19):

 
Figure 19. Marking the corners

Knowing all this, we use the formula to find the length X = orange (30) × 50 = 1/√3 × 50 = 50/√3 ( Figure 20).

 
Figure 20. Length determination

Now let's go back to the whole picture and consider another triangle, one of the sides of which is the radius of the circle (Figure 21). We know that the blue lines divide the squares in half, which means that the lower part of the triangle is 50 meters. At the bottom we know that it is a rectangle, that is, the green side of the triangle is also 100 m.
 
Figure 21. Determining the radius of a circle

It turns out that we have a right-angled triangle with legs of 50 meters and (100+50/√3) meters, and we need to find the length of the hypotenuse. According to the Pythagorean theorem, the sum of the squares of its legs is equal to the square of the hypotenuse: a2 + b2 = c2. Let's put our values here and solve the equation:
50² + (100 + 50/√3)² = r².    r = √(50² + (100 + 50/√3)²)
r = √(2500 + (100 +28,86)²) = √(2500 +16606,83) = 138,22.

The radius of the circle bordering all territories (138 meters) turned out to be less than the radius of the router (130 meters). This means that the brothers must either get a more powerful router, or place all their equipment closer to the router.

Railway station 
Foundation of the historical building was laid in the period 1900-1905 for the construction of the Orenburg-Tashkent highway. Subsequently, a new 3-storey train station was built in 1969. According to historical records, the Russian Emperor Nicholas II has founded idea of the construction of the railway station, which was one of the most beautiful buildings in Kyzylorda, named after Perovsky. The station was distinguished by incredibly beautiful architecture and quality. There is a legend that in Orenburg, red bricks were made and supplied for this station, and when pouring bricks, horsehair was added to increase its strength. The total length of the mainline railway network passing through the Kyzylorda region is 1051.7 km. There are 8 railway stations serving passengers in the region. 
Task .  Part of the Kyzylorda railway station consists of two floors. Width of this building is 73 meters. The first floor occupies 60% of the total width of the building, and the second floor occupies the remaining 40%.
1. What is the width of the first floor of the station?
2. What is the width of the second floor of the station?
3. If the height of each floor is 5 meters, then what is the total floor area of the station?
Note:corners of the station building are rectangular and equal to the width of the floor on each floor. 

Solution:
1.The first floor occupies 60% of the entire width of the building, so its width is 73 × 0.6 =43.8 m. 
2. The second floor occupies the remaining 40% of the width of the building, hence its width 73 × 0.4 =29.2 m.
	3. Total floor area of the station is equal to the area of the first floor and the area of the second floor. The area of each floor is equal to the product of its width and height: the area of the first floor: 43.8×5=219 m2.
The area of the second floor: 29.2×5=146 m2.
The total floor area of the station: 219+146=365 m2. Therefore, we calculated the width of each floor and the total floor area of the station.
Answer: width of the first floor is 43.8 m; width of the second floor is 29.2 m; total floor area is 365 m2.
